# Recovery & Injury Prevention

## Post-Session Recovery Protocols

*Sources: tennisfitness.com Ultimate Tennis Recovery Guide (Mar 2025); USTA Nutrition for Recovery; USTA SoCal Recovery Like a Pro (Jul 2025)*

**Immediate post-session (0-30 minutes):**
- Light jogging or cycling 5-10 min to flush lactic acid
- Protein + carb intake within 30 minutes (shake + banana; cottage cheese + fruit)
- Rehydrate with water or coconut water; add electrolytes after heavy sweat
- Ice shoulder 10-15 min if sore or after high-volume serve sessions

**Same-day recovery (1-4 hours):**
- Full meal within 2 hours: carb + protein + healthy fats
- Top recovery foods (USTA): tart cherries, blueberries (quercetin), salmon (vitamin D), ginger, beets (blood flow)
- Static stretching: 20-45 sec holds — hamstring, quad, hip flexor, calf, tricep, shoulder, lumbar twist

**Cold therapy:** Ice bath at 10-12C (50-54F), 3 x 2-min sets. Below 10C adds no benefit and risks excessive vasoconstriction.

**Sleep — the highest-priority recovery tool:** 7-9 hours minimum; elite athletes target 10 hours. One night of poor sleep impairs reaction time, sprint speed, and decision-making.

## Periodization & Volume Management

*Source: tennisacademy.app Serve Volume Blueprint 2026*

| Player Level | Off-season serves/week | In-season serves/week | Serve days/week |
|---|---|---|---|
| Adult recreational | 120-240 | 160-300 (incl. match) | 2-3 |
| Junior 12-16U | 180-300 | 150-280 | 3-4 |
| Junior 10U | 100-180 | 80-160 | 3 |

**Critical rules:**
- Increase weekly load by no more than 5-10% per week
- Never serve at high intensity on consecutive days
- Every 4th week: deload (40% volume reduction) — non-negotiable
- After illness/break: restart at lower base, don't jump to previous peak

**Tournament taper:** Heaviest serve day falls 4-6 days before tournament. Reduce volume 30-40% the week before competition.

**Overtraining signs:** Persistent shoulder stiffness &gt;24hrs, loss of serve velocity despite practice, disturbed sleep, recurring minor injuries.

## Shoulder Injury Prevention

The rotator cuff (Supraspinatus, Infraspinatus, Teres Minor, Subscapularis) is the priority structure. Training frequency: 3x/week, scheduled away from court sessions.

### Rotator Cuff Strengthening

| Exercise | Sets x Reps | Key Cue |
|---|---|---|
| External rotation at side (elbow 90) | 3 x 12-15 | Scapula retracted and depressed throughout |
| Internal rotation at side | 3 x 12-15 | Same scapular set |
| 90/90 external rotation (abducted to shoulder height) | 3 x 10-12 | Mimics serve cocking position exactly |
| 90/90 internal rotation | 3 x 10-12 | Trains eccentric deceleration muscles |
| High row (band) | 3 x 12 | Lead with elbow, not wrist |

### Endurance & Fatigue Management

Serve mechanics deteriorate under fatigue: trunk disengages first, arm compensates, shoulder injury risk concentrates in deceleration.

- **Eccentric deceleration training:** External rotation lowering slowly 3-4 counts, 3 x 8 reps
- **Rotator cuff endurance:** High-rep band sets 2-3 x 20
- **Interval serve programming:** Vary intensity — technical (60-70%), conditioning (85-90%), peak (95%). No session at 100% daily.

### Chronic Adaptation Risk

Elite players gain external rotation (ER) ROM at the expense of internal rotation (IR). Loss of 10-15 degrees total rotation ROM documented. Monitor and address with:

- Sleeper stretch (serving arm): 2 min hold
- Cross-body stretch: 2 min/side
- Post-session shoulder icing if any soreness

### Common Injury Sources

| Injury | Cause | Prevention |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Shoulder impingement | Toss too close to 12 o'clock | Keep toss slightly lateral |
| Rotator cuff strain | Insufficient deceleration training | Eccentric external rotation work |
| Tennis elbow | High-tension full poly strings | Switch to hybrid or lower tension |
| Lower back stress | Excessive hyperextension | Core stability, not just flexibility |

## Post-Serve Cool-down Protocol (10 min, holds 90-120+ sec)

1. Sleeper stretch (2 min/side)
2. Cross-body stretch (2 min/side)
3. Doorway chest at 90°, 120°, 150° (90 sec each)
4. Hip flexor lunge with overhead reach (2 min/side)
5. Side-lying thoracic rotation (2 min/side)
